Framework

How Finance Teams Think Through Problems

A consulting-style workflow for approaching any financial question systematically. Click each stage to explore.

Stage 01
Question
Stage 02
Investigation
Stage 03
Report
Stage 04
Analysis
Stage 05
Decision
Define the Business Question
Every FDS session starts here. Before opening a report, be precise about what you're actually trying to answer. Vague questions produce unfocused analysis. Sharpen the question — then select the report.
Example: "Why is the balance negative?" → More specific: "Is this deficit driven by payroll, a specific expense category, a timing issue, or missing funding?"
Narrow the Investigation Path
Determine which domain the issue likely lives in: payroll, procurement, project spending, timing, or funding. This narrows which report category to open first and prevents pulling unnecessary detail too early.
Example: If payroll is suspected → go to Payroll Reports first. If open POs are suspected → go to Procurement Reports first.
Select the Right Report
Start at the summary level. The Project Summary Statement or SPFR Summary gives context before drilling down. Only move to detail reports when the summary raises a specific flag that requires further investigation.
Example: SPFR Summary → sees consultants category at 110% → opens SPFR Detail → drills to specific vendors driving overage.
Analyze and Locate the Driver
Once in the report, focus on the data that answers the original question. Avoid getting distracted by adjacent data. Use the Trial Balance for transaction-level detail. Use Payroll Detail for employee-level salary confirmation.
Example: Trial Balance confirms a large journal entry posted in the wrong period — that's the driver of the apparent deficit.
Determine Corrective Action
With the issue confirmed, determine the corrective path: cost transfer, budget amendment, journal entry correction, or communication with the responsible party. FDS informs the decision — it does not make it.
Example: Payroll charged to wrong ChartString → initiate cost transfer → monitor SPFR next month to confirm correction posted.

Then Use

Then Use

PO Open Commitment — identify which POs are still open and affecting your balance
Then Use

Resolution

Start With
Trial Balance by Account Detail — identify the transaction date and accounting period
Then Use
Chartfield Transaction Statement — confirm the voucher or journal entry date posted correctly
Resolution
If timing is confirmed as an error, initiate journal entry correction with Finance

Start With
SPFR Summary by Budget Category — find which category is at or over budget
Then Use
SPFR Detail — drill to the specific charges driving the overage in that category
Also Check
Payroll Summary — confirm salary encumbrances are not inflating the committed amount

📂
Open the SPFR Summary by Budget Category
The project appears overspent. Start with the SPFR Summary — never skip straight to detail. The summary gives you the full picture: budget, actuals, commitments, and % used across all expense categories. This is your diagnostic starting point.
💰
Review the Budget Balance Available Column
Check the "Budget Balance Available" column. Negative numbers show in red — those are your problem categories. Note which budget categories are at or over 100% used. Salary & Wages and Consultants are the most common culprits.
🔗
Compare Actuals Against Commitments
Look at the commitments column alongside actuals. If encumbrances are high, the available balance may show negative even if the actual spending is within budget. Distinguish between what has posted and what is still committed but unspent.
👤
Navigate to Payroll Detail Report
If Salary & Wages is the flagged category, drill directly to the Payroll Detail Report. From the SPFR, you can use the navigation links at the top of the report to move directly to Payroll Detail without re-entering parameters.
✓
Validate Payroll Charges by Employee
In the Payroll Detail, review each employee's charges — confirm name, combo code, earn code, pay period dates, salary, and fringe. Look for employees who shouldn't be on this project, incorrect earn codes, or fringe rates that look off.
⚖️
Open the Trial Balance by Account Detail
Return to the SPFR navigation and open the Trial Balance. This will show every transaction — not just payroll — that hit the project's ChartString. Look for unexpected journal entries, large vouchers, or misposted charges.
🎯
Identify the Primary Expense Driver
In the Trial Balance, sort by amount descending to surface the largest charges. Identify the specific transaction — journal ID, voucher number, or payroll run — that is driving the overage. This is the fact-finding moment. Confirm before acting.
✅
Confirm Resolution and Monitor
Once corrective action is taken — cost transfer, journal entry, or budget amendment — monitor the SPFR in the following month to confirm the correction posted correctly. Document the analysis. The workflow is complete when the SPFR shows the corrected balance.
